# Top 10 Must-Watch YouTube Channels for AI Learners

These channels are consistently high quality, accessible to beginners, and cover the topics in this guide. Watch these and you'll accelerate your AI understanding faster than any course.

---

## 1. Andrej Karpathy

**Channel:** youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy  
**Skill level:** Intermediate–Advanced  
**Best video to start:** "Let's build GPT: from scratch, in code, spelled out"

Andrej is one of the world's best AI educators — former Director of AI at Tesla, founding member of OpenAI. His tutorials build neural networks from first principles with extraordinary clarity. If you want to truly understand how LLMs work under the hood, this channel is unmissable.

**Must-watch series:**
- Neural Networks: Zero to Hero (full playlist)
- "Intro to Large Language Models" (1-hour lecture)

---

## 2. 3Blue1Brown

**Channel:** youtube.com/@3blue1brown  
**Skill level:** Beginner–Intermediate  
**Best video to start:** "But what is a neural network?" (Neural Networks chapter 1)

Grant Sanderson's mathematical animations make abstract concepts visual and intuitive. His series on neural networks and the "Attention in Transformers" video are the clearest visual explanations of AI foundations available anywhere.

**Must-watch:**
- Neural networks playlist
- "Attention in Transformers, visually explained"

---

## 3. Two Minute Papers

**Channel:** youtube.com/@TwoMinutePapers  
**Skill level:** All levels  
**Best video to start:** Any recent video — they're all short

Károly Zsolnai-Fehér makes 3-5 minute videos summarising AI research papers. Perfect for staying current on what's happening in AI research without reading papers yourself. His enthusiasm is infectious.

**Best for:** Weekly AI research news in digestible form

---

## 4. Yannic Kilcher

**Channel:** youtube.com/@YannicKilcher  
**Skill level:** Intermediate–Advanced  
**Best video to start:** "GPT-3: Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (Paper Explained)"

Deep-dives on important AI research papers. Yannic reads through papers live and explains them in detail. Excellent for ML practitioners who want to understand the research behind the tools they use.

**Best for:** Understanding landmark AI papers in depth

---

## 5. AI Explained

**Channel:** youtube.com/@aiexplained-official  
**Skill level:** Beginner–Intermediate  
**Best video to start:** Browse recent uploads — all relevant to current AI landscape

Accessible explanations of AI developments, model comparisons, and capability demonstrations. Does excellent side-by-side comparisons of different LLMs. Great for IT professionals who want to understand capabilities without needing deep ML knowledge.

**Best for:** Practical AI tool evaluations and explanations

---

## 6. Matt Wolfe

**Channel:** youtube.com/@mreflow  
**Skill level:** Beginner  
**Best video to start:** His latest "AI tools roundup" video

Matt covers the practical AI tools landscape — new releases, comparisons, tutorials for non-technical users. If you want to know what new AI tools are available every week, this is the best source.

**Best for:** Discovering new AI tools; non-technical audience

---

## 7. Fireship

**Channel:** youtube.com/@Fireship  
**Skill level:** Intermediate  
**Best video to start:** "I tried 10 AI coding tools. Here's what actually works."

Fast, funny, technical. Fireship covers AI through a developer lens — code, tools, frameworks, and the industry in 5-10 minute videos. His "100 seconds of X" format is brilliant for quick concept introductions.

**Best for:** Developers; AI coding tools; fast-paced format

---

## 8. Sam Witteveen

**Channel:** youtube.com/@samwitteveenai  
**Skill level:** Intermediate–Advanced  
**Best video to start:** Any tutorial on LangChain, RAG, or AI agents

Practical, code-first tutorials on building AI applications. Covers LangChain, RAG, embeddings, agents, and API usage in detail. If you want to go from understanding AI to building with it, Sam's channel bridges the gap.

**Best for:** Developers building LLM applications; tutorials with real code

---

## 9. Sentdex

**Channel:** youtube.com/@sentdex  
**Skill level:** Beginner–Intermediate  
**Best video to start:** Python/ML fundamentals playlist

Harrison Kinsley has been teaching Python and machine learning on YouTube for over a decade. His style is friendly and practical. Good foundational channel for those who need to build Python and ML basics before tackling LLMs.

**Best for:** Python fundamentals; machine learning basics; long-form tutorials

---

## 10. Google DeepMind

**Channel:** youtube.com/@Google_DeepMind  
**Skill level:** All levels  
**Best video to start:** AlphaFold documentary, or any "Research at DeepMind" video

The official channel from one of the world's leading AI research labs. Includes research explainers, podcast episodes, and documentary-style videos on breakthrough AI research. High production quality and authoritative content.

**Best for:** Understanding frontier AI research from the source; inspiration

---

## Honourable Mentions

- **Lex Fridman** — Long-form interviews with AI leaders (3-hour episodes; rich depth)
- **Sebastian Lague** — Creative coding + AI projects (inspiring for visual learners)
- **StatQuest with Josh Starmer** — Statistics and ML concepts explained simply (essential foundations)
- **DeepLearning.AI** — Structured courses on YouTube from Andrew Ng's organisation

---

## How to Use This List

**Complete beginner:** Start with 3Blue1Brown (foundations) → Matt Wolfe (tools) → AI Explained (current landscape)

**Developer wanting to build:** Start with Fireship (tooling) → Sam Witteveen (tutorials) → Andrej Karpathy (deep understanding)

**Researcher/academic:** Yannic Kilcher (papers) → Two Minute Papers (news) → Google DeepMind (frontier research)
