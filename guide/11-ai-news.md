# 11 — Staying Current: The Best AI News Sources

AI is moving faster than any other technology field. Something that's frontier today may be superseded in 6 months. Here's how to stay informed without drowning in noise.

---

## The Problem with AI News

There's too much of it. Every week brings:
- New model releases
- Research paper announcements
- Startup funding rounds
- Opinion pieces claiming AI will save or destroy the world

Good sources filter signal from noise and explain why things matter, not just what happened.

---

## Email Newsletters (Recommended Starting Point)

### The Batch (deeplearning.ai)
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Author:** Andrew Ng (Coursera co-founder, former head of Google Brain)  
**Style:** Curated digest of the week's AI developments with thoughtful commentary  
**Best for:** Balanced technical + business perspective; excellent for IT professionals  
**Subscribe:** deeplearning.ai/the-batch

### TLDR AI
**Frequency:** Daily  
**Style:** Short, scannable bullets — 5-minute read  
**Best for:** Staying aware of daily developments without deep dives  
**Subscribe:** tldr.tech/ai

### Ben's Bites
**Frequency:** Daily  
**Author:** Ben Tossell  
**Style:** Curated links with brief summaries; strong on practical tools and products  
**Best for:** Discovering new AI tools and applications  
**Subscribe:** bensbites.co

### Import AI (Jack Clark)
**Frequency:** Weekly  
**Author:** Jack Clark (Anthropic co-founder)  
**Style:** Technical research focus; in-depth analysis of AI papers  
**Best for:** Those who want to go deeper on the research side  
**Subscribe:** jack-clark.net

### Stratechery
**Frequency:** Multiple times per week (partly paywalled)  
**Author:** Ben Thompson  
**Style:** Strategic/business analysis of technology including AI  
**Best for:** Understanding the business and strategic implications of AI  
**Subscribe:** stratechery.com

## Podcasts

### Lex Fridman Podcast
Long-form interviews with AI researchers, engineers, and scientists. Best for depth on specific people and topics. Episodes often run 3+ hours.

### The TWIML AI Podcast (This Week in Machine Learning)
Technical interviews with ML researchers and practitioners. Consistent quality over many years.

### AI Explained (YouTube-first)
Accessible breakdowns of AI developments and research papers. Great for building foundational understanding.

### Latent Space
Technical podcast for AI engineers and researchers. Covers infrastructure, models, and applied AI in depth.

### No Priors
Short, sharp podcast from Sequoia Capital. Business and product focus on AI startups and trends.

## Research Papers (Advanced)

If you want to read primary sources:

- **arXiv.org** (cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.CL sections) — Preprint papers before peer review
- **Papers With Code** (paperswithcode.com) — Papers linked to open-source implementations
- **Semantic Scholar** — AI-powered research discovery

**Tip:** You don't need to read full papers. The abstract, introduction, and conclusion of a well-written paper tell you 80% of what you need to know.

---

## Staying Current: A Practical System

You don't need to read everything. Here's a sustainable approach:

1. **Daily (5 minutes):** Scan TLDR AI headlines while having your morning coffee
2. **Weekly (30 minutes):** Read The Batch for curated weekly digest
3. **Monthly (1-2 hours):** Deep-dive on one topic that's relevant to your current work
4. **Quarterly:** Review what tools you're using — are there better options now?

**The most important habit:** Apply what you learn. Reading about AI without using it doesn't build real understanding. Pick one tool and use it for real work.
